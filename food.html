Scottish cuisine combines tradition and quality local ingredients:

Haggis

Haggis is Scotland’s national dish, traditionally made from sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with oatmeal, onions, suet, and spices. It’s often served with “neeps and tatties” (mashed turnips and potatoes) and whisky, especially on Burns Night.

Scottish Salmon

Renowned worldwide, Scottish salmon comes from the clean rivers and lochs of Scotland. It’s smoked, grilled, or poached, and prized for its delicate flavor and firm pink flesh.

Shortbread

Shortbread is a buttery, crumbly biscuit made with just flour, butter, and sugar. Once a festive treat, it is now enjoyed all year round with tea or coffee.

Scotch Pies

Scotch pies are small, double-crust meat pies, traditionally filled with minced mutton or beef. They are a classic snack, especially popular at football matches and fairs.

Scotch Whisky

Scotland’s most famous drink, Scotch whisky is made from malted barley and aged in oak barrels. Each region has its own style, from smoky Islay to smooth Speyside.
